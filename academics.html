Our Curriculum

How we run things

By the kids, for the kids.

Little Acorns places a strong emphasis on independence and free choice. Children are encouraged to explore their environment freely and become contributing members of our community. A number of our daily activities will be designed to encourage the Montessori concepts of self-help and independence. We value the core ideals of Montessori and offer a number of Montessori materials and lessons alongside inspiration from Reggio-Emilia.

Here at Little Acorns Education Center, we truly value the process over the product. We encourage and facilitate creative play and activities. In practice, this means offering open-ended materials as often as possible. Children are presented with materials and are encourage to freely explore and create.

Our center is licensed and operates as a family group daycare. This allows us to offer a small family environment with a mixed-age classroom. Mixed-age classrooms are utilized frequently in the Montessori curriculum. This provides the students the opportunity to learn from each other, much like a community.

Our Daily Rhythm

Keeping with our philosophy of extended uninterrupted blocks of time, activities may be moved or altered to fit the flow of the day. Below are the main components of our day:

  • A group gathering time each morning with the children and teachers to discuss the day and any other important information.
  • Work time (choice time): children are encouraged to make a choice from any area of the room. Teachers observe and engage with the children, offering support as needed.
  • Invitation to Play: This is planned by the teacher based on the observed children’s interests. The goal is to engage the children in collaborative and imaginative play. Some examples include: bakery, tower building, and construction worker.
  • Presented Works: during this time, the teacher will setup a particular activity geared for a specific skillset and invite the children over to participate individually or in small groups, depending on the activity. The other children will be encouraged to freely participate in other areas of the room until their turn.
  • Outside play time at a minimum of twice a day.
  • Regularly scheduled meal and rest times.

ACADEMIC DETAILS

EMERGENT LITERATURE

Little Acorns promotes reading and writing throughout the day. Group and individual reading times are encouraged and built into the daily schedule. Books are available throughout the day. Books can be seen throughout the classroom and can be used in various ways. For example, a book with pictures of buildings can be used in the block area. There are writing materials readily available to any child, including sandpaper letters and numbers. Labels with the written word and picture are placed throughout the classroom.

MESSY ACTIVITIES

A messy day is a good day! Little Acorns does not shy away from messy activities but rather embrace them. It is our belief that clothes can always be changed and messes are part of childhood.

BABYWEARING

Little Acorns recognizes the benefits of proper babywearing. Babywearing allows for the infant or child to remain in close contact with their caregiver while allowing the caregiver to remain hands-free and mobile. This is particularly helpful when babies do not want to be put down, when teething, or when a child is just having an “off” day. All staff members are aware of proper babywearing techniques. All carriers that are used in the center are CPSIA complaint and inspected often to ensure proper operation and safety.

YEARLY PORTFOLIOS

Every enrolled child will receive a personal learning portfolio in August. This portfolio will highlight their learning journey from September – June and will include: art work, pictures, observations, and assessment results.

COMING SOON!

Little Acorns will be adding a resource library! Families will be encouraged to checkout one learning item a month to take home and share with their family. Some items may include: STEAM kits, puppets, felt stories, manipulatives, and board games

We sincerely hope this resource library serves as a starting point for more family time! With new activities and the excitement of sharing school with home, we hope your evenings and weekends are filled with more family fun!
